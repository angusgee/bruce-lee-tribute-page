Bruce Lee

The man, the myth, the legend - RIP

Bruce Lee, born in 1940 in San Francisco and raised in Hong Kong, was a martial artist, actor, and cultural icon whose influence transcended borders. Known for his pioneering approach to martial arts and philosophy, Lee founded his own style, Jeet Kune Do, blending various combat techniques. He gained fame with his roles in The Green Hornet and classic martial arts films like Enter the Dragon. Beyond his cinematic legacy, Lee's philosophies on self-expression and physical mastery continue to inspire millions. Despite his untimely death at 32, his impact on martial arts and popular culture endures globally.

  • 1940 - Born in San Francisco, California, while his parents were on tour with a Chinese opera.
  • 1941 - Moves back to Hong Kong with his family.
  • 1946 - Begins acting in Hong Kong cinema as a child, appearing in over 20 films by the time he's 18.
  • 1953 - Starts training in Wing Chun under Master Ip Man, developing his passion for martial arts.
  • 1958 - Wins the Hong Kong Cha Cha Championship, showing his versatility beyond martial arts.
  • 1959 - Moves back to the United States, settling in Seattle. Works in a restaurant and begins teaching martial arts.
  • 1961 - Enrolls at the University of Washington, where he studies philosophy and continues teaching martial arts.
  • 1963 - Opens his first martial arts school, the "Jun Fan Gung Fu Institute," in Seattle.
  • 1964 - Moves to Oakland and opens his second martial arts school. Demonstrates his martial arts skills at the Long Beach International Karate Championships, gaining widespread attention.
  • 1965 - Returns to Hong Kong after the death of his father. Marries Linda Emery and has his first child, Brandon Lee.
  • 1966 - Gains fame in the U.S. as Kato in the TV series *The Green Hornet*.
  • 1969 - His second child, Shannon Lee, is born. Suffers a severe back injury while training, leading to a long recovery period and deepened philosophical reflection on martial arts.
  • 1971 - Moves back to Hong Kong and signs a contract with Golden Harvest, launching his film career in Asia.
  • 1972 - Stars in *Fist of Fury* and *The Way of the Dragon*, the latter of which he writes, directs, and stars in. The film features an iconic fight scene with Chuck Norris.
  • 1973 - Completes *Enter the Dragon*, which becomes his most famous film. Dies unexpectedly in Hong Kong on July 20, aged 32.
  • 1973 - *Enter the Dragon* premieres a month after his death, becoming a global hit and solidifying his legacy as a martial arts legend.
